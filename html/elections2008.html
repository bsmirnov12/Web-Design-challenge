2008 Elections

The 40th Canadian General Election, that was held on Tuesday, October 14, 2008, after the previous parliament had been dissolved by the Governor General on September 7, 2008 on the advice of the Prime Minister.
Stephen Harper and the Conservative Party form minority government. Liberals continue to lose seats. Bloc Québécois still feels strong (but not for long).
This parliament was notable for a coalition between the Liberal Party and NDP that led to the motion of non-confidence, that, in turn, led to dissolution of the parliament and the next elections of 2011.
