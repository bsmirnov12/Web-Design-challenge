New Democratic Party

The chart shows a very important event in the history of New Democratic Party - in 2011, for the firs time, it became Official Opposition moving up from the forth place and pushing aside Liberal Party and Bloc Québécois. However this success wasn't long lasting and this left-oriented party lost its positions presumably mostly to Liberal Party for the most part. Its popularity is in the decline and today it is even lower then Bloc Québécois (recent outsider).

Gains and Losses

These charts show how many seats the party lost or gained when comparing two consecuitive elections, as well as its rival parties that won or lost the seats. The comparison is done riding by riding, and not all consecuitive elections are comparable - in 2013 Elections Canada redefined boundaries of almost all electoral districts, increasing its number from 308 to 338.
