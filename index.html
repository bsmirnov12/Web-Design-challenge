Changes in Canadian Political Landscape

This project explores 15 years of canadian political history, showing ups and downs of major political players, and swings of public political attitude.

In order to make this demonstration, I pulled elections results from Elections Canada web site. The depth of exploration is limited by year 2004, because earlier data (years 2000 and 1996) was in different file format and given limited time for the task these results were not included in the analysis.

After pulling report tables from the web site the data was normalized: earlier tables missed FED codes, candidate party affiliation was embedded in a candidate description field, naming of parties changed from year to year, there were some other inconsistenses and formatting errors. The result of normalization was a set of tables in unified format containing information about party affiliation of each participating candidate, riding, number of votes. These tables are available for review via Data menu.

The two sets of charts were generated using Matplotlib:

Political parties
These charts demonstrate evolutions of success (or failure) of the parties that have representatives in the House of Commons.
Elections Years
These charts show political layouts in the House of Commons after the elections.
